ABOUT

I am a recent biomedical engineering graduate from Professor Adam Feinberg's lab at Carnegie Mellon University where I bioprinted complex 3D in vitro tissue models. I have extensive experience in additive manufacturing, biofabrication, microfluidics, cell culture, device prototyping, computer-aided design (CAD), and in vitro tissue systems. I fabricated and cultured a multitude of tissue types including cardiovascular, skeletal muscle, hepatic, and pancreatic. The main thesis research was focused on engineering structured cardiac organoids with FRESH 3D bioprinting for increased reproducibility and control cellular organization. I am currently seeking a full-time R&D position beginning in fall 2025 where I can applying my interdisciplinary skills to impactful technologies in the regenerative medicine, biotechnology, and tissue engineering fields.
