ABOUT

I am a recent biomedical engineering graduate from Professor Adam Feinberg's lab at Carnegie Mellon University where I bioprinted complex 3D in vitro tissue models. I have extensive experience in additive manufacturing, biofabrication, microfluidics, cell culture, device prototyping, computer-aided design (CAD), and in vitro tissue systems. I fabricated and cultured a multitude of tissue types including cardiovascular, skeletal muscle, hepatic, and pancreatic. My main thesis research was focused on engineering structured cardiac organoids with FRESH 3D bioprinting for increased reproducibility and controlled cellular organization. I am currently seeking a full-time R&D position beginning in fall 2025 where I can applying my interdisciplinary skills to impactful technologies in the regenerative medicine, biotechnology, and tissue engineering fields.

3D Printed Scaffolds of Fibrillar Collagen Isoforms

Collagen scaffolds printed from various fibrillar collagen isoforms, specifically collagen types I, II, III and an equal part combination of all three. (A) G-Code depicting the print pathing of a 3D grid with a square cross-section of 10 mm × 10 mm and 2 mm in height. Stereo microscope images of the bioprinted (B,C) collagen I, (D,E) collagen II, (F,G) collagen III, and (H,I) combinatorial collagen scaffolds and a zoomed-in ROI. All scale bars are 2000 μm. FRESH 3D Bioprinted Structured Muscle Aggregates

Micrographs of printed structured muscle aggreggates from the day of printing to a fully condensed state from cell-mediated compaction approximately 10 days later. Maximum intensity projections depicting nuclei (red), actin (green), and collagen (white) of strucutred muscle aggregates corresponding to the above micrograph. Micrograph scale bars = 1 mm. Maximum intensity projection scale bars = 500 μm.

Printed Muscle Aggregates in Custom Culturing Vessel

Culturing of multiple individual printed muscle aggregates in custom PDMS culture vessel enabling rapid printing and culture in the same location.

Minimal Print Size of Fabricated Cell Aggregates

Reference image depicting the miniscule size of the fabricated muscle aggregates with FRESH 3D bioprinting as the red print is placed on top of a single finger in a green lab glove.

Cellular Alignment in Structured Muscle Aggregates

Maximum intensity projections of a compacted structured muscle aggregates showing the actin cytoskeleton (green) aligned parallel to the internal collagen rod. Aggregate scale bar = 500 μm. ROI scale bar = 100 μm.
